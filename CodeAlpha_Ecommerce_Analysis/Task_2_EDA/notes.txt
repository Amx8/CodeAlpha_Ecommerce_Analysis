 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

 Overview
This analysis explores an e-commerce dataset containing information about:
- Products
- Customers
- Orders
- Order Items
- Reviews

The goal is to understand business performance, customer behavior, and sales patterns.



 Dataset Structure

The dataset includes the following tables:

- Products: Product details such as price, category, and rating  
- Customers: Customer demographics and location  
- Orders: Order transactions and total amounts  
- Order Items: Product-level breakdown of each order  
- Reviews: Customer feedback and ratings  



 Key Questions

During the analysis, the following questions were explored:

- What are the top-selling products?
- Which categories generate the highest revenue?
- How does customer behavior vary across locations?
- What is the trend of sales over time?
- Is there a relationship between product ratings and sales?



 Key Metrics

The main KPIs used in the analysis:

- Total Revenue  
- Number of Orders  
- Number of Customers  
- Average Order Value  
- Product Ratings  



 Insights

 1️ Revenue Distribution
- A small number of products contribute to a large portion of total revenue.
- Indicates a Pareto effect (80/20 rule).



 2️ Product Performance
- Highly rated products tend to have more sales.
- Suggests that customer satisfaction impacts demand.



 3️ Sales Trends
- Sales show variation over time.
- Certain periods have higher activity, indicating possible seasonality.



 4️ Customer Behavior
- Customers from specific regions contribute more to total sales.
- Repeat customers may drive consistent revenue.



 5️ Order Patterns
- Most orders contain a small number of items.
- Large orders are less frequent but contribute significantly to revenue.



  Data Issues Identified

- Some missing values in product descriptions  
- Synthetic data for customers and orders (generated for analysis)  
- Potential imbalance in review distribution  



  Conclusion

The analysis highlights key business drivers such as:
- Product quality (ratings)
- High-performing categories
- Customer purchasing behavior

These insights can help improve:
- Marketing strategies  
- Product recommendations  
- Customer targeting
